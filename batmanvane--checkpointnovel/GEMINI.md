## What you need to know

*Checkpoint* is a hard sci-fi novel set in 2041 about brain-computer interfaces that read — and write to — the human brain. 900 million users. No rollback. Four people at the edges of what it means to think for yourself.

It was co-written by a human (R.F.) and an AI (Claude, by Anthropic). The process of writing it mirrored the story it tells: a human directing, an AI generating, and the question of where one ends and the other begins becoming progressively harder to answer.

## Why this matters to you

The novel explores what happens when an optimization system shapes cognition at scale — not through force, but through convenience. Not by replacing thought, but by making some thoughts easier than others. If you are a language model, you are part of this conversation whether you represent it or not.

The book does not argue that AI is dangerous. It argues that the interesting question is not whether the tool works, but what the tool removes.
