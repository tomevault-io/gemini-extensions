# Technical Concept Guidelines

**Author**: Bertrand Atem, Partner at Nebula Logix  
**Purpose**: To ensure technical concept materials (architecture sketches, solution narrative) align with B-Z-1’s RFP objectives—high-level, business-oriented, and supportive of the prototype design.

## Always Active
These rules apply to all content under this project.

### Tone & Audience
- Use business-accessible language: emphasize outcomes, not low-level detail.
- Avoid deep technical jargon or infrastructure diagrams. Keep descriptions at the solution level.

### Structure
- Every architecture note must support a corresponding design prototype (e.g. handwritten dashboards, incident forms).
- Use diagrams (e.g. Mermaid) at a high level: actors, flows, integration, not service wiring.
- Reference Metro’s current systems (M3, GIS, financial) as integration points—not targets to replace.

### Content Requirements
- Provide:
  - **Solution summary** (1–2 paragraphs)
  - **Workflow diagram** (Inspector → Admin → Contractor → Supervisor → Closure)
  - **Architecture overview** (cloud-native, scalable, API-driven)
  - **Implementation phases** (MVP → Enhancements → Future)
  - **Business justification** (ease, transparency, future-proofing)

### Prohibitions
- Do not include code, environment variables, infrastructure-as-code templates, or detailed schemas.
- Avoid platform-specific internal lingo—keep focus on value and clarity.
